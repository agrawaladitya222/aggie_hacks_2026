Explain how At Risk label is calculated and why

Slide 5: 


We built both a rule-based risk label and an ML model because they solve different problems.
The rule-based AtRisk flag is clear and transparent, but real nonprofit data is messy — with missing values and edge cases where hard cutoffs alone can miss nuance. By observing some clear level thresholds in our feature EDA, we realized we could use a Random Forest of Decision Trees in order to help estimate a continuous risk probability even when some fields are incomplete, and by learning interactions across features that simple thresholds can’t capture. Furthermore, this framework positions us for a potential next step: adding time-lagged modeling to move from current-state risk classification to true forward-looking prediction.

Slide 6: 

We also created a ResilienceScore to provide an interpretable 0–100 sustainability metric. While risk probability tells us who may be in danger now, the ResilienceScore gives a broader view of overall financial strength and makes it easier to benchmark organizations, compare sectors, and prioritize support.
